Second wave of COVID-19 likely in January-February: WHO

The WHO council has said that it's likely that the state will be hit by the second wave of COVID-19 in January-February based on what is happening in Europe. The WHO has asked the authorities to ensure there is no complacency in testing and said that labs should be functional in every district and municipal corporation limits.

Canadians can celebrate Christmas if COVID-19 contained now: Trudeau

Canada's PM Justin Trudeau has said Christmas celebrations in the country will depend on "what we do in the coming days and weeks". He added that provinces and territories will have to make "difficult decisions to shut down things now so that things don't get too bad in the coming months". Canada has reported over 2.9 lakh COVID-19 cases.

World may be tired, but COVID-19 is not tired of us: WHO chief

The WHO's Director-General Tedros Adhanom on Monday said, "We might be tired of COVID-19, but it's not tired of us." He added, "It preys on those weaker than us, but it preys on other divisions too. There's no vaccine for poverty, hunger, climate change, and inequality." He spoke from quarantine after coming in contact with someone who tested COVID-19 positive.

PM of India speaks to WHO chief, discusses coordinated global response to COVID-19

Prime Minister Narendra Modi held a telephonic conversation with WHO chief Tedros Ghebreyesus and appreciated WHO's important role in facilitating coordinated global response to COVID-19, the PM's Office said. The WHO chief stressed close and regular collaboration between the organisation and Indian health authorities, and appreciated India's initiatives like Ayushman Bharat scheme, the PMO added.

Pfizer says its COVID-19 vaccine is more than 90% effective

Pfizer today said its experimental vaccine was more than 90% effective in preventing COVID-19 based on initial data from a large study. Pfizer and German partner BioNTech SE are the first drugmakers to show successful data from a large-scale clinical trial of a coronavirus vaccine. “Today is a great day for science and humanity,” Pfizer’s chief executive Albert Bourla said.

Up to 40% of population is in high risk group for COVID-19: Germany

Germany's Health Minister Jens Spahn said that up to 40% of the country's population is in the "high risk" group for coronavirus. The group includes people with diabetes, high blood pressure, obesity and those above the age of 60, he added. Germany recently imposed a partial lockdown to curb the surge in coronavirus cases.

Global coronavirus death toll exceeds 12 lakh-mark

The global death toll from coronavirus on Monday rose to 12,02,081, according to the Johns Hopkins University tracker. The US has reported the highest COVID-19 deaths at 2,31,011, followed by Brazil at 1,60,074 and India at 1,22,607, the data showed. The total cases of coronavirus in the world rose to over 4.66 crore.

Reduce social contacts by 25%: Canada as coronavirus cases rise

Canada's Public Health Agency has asked citizens to reduce their social contacts by 25% as COVID-19 cases continue to surge in the country. To "bend the epidemic curve" it's necessary to reduce the number of contacts as much as possible, the country's chief public health officer said. Canada on Friday recorded its biggest one-day tally with 3,457 new COVID-19 cases.

Asia becomes 2nd region in world to cross 1 crore COVID-19 cases

Asia became the second region in the world to exceed 1 crore coronavirus cases on Saturday, according to a Reuters tally. Asia accounts for about one-fourth of the 4.2 crore global cases and 14% of global death toll. Within the region, South Asia, which is led by India, is the worst-affected as it accounts for nearly 21% of global caseload.

What is WHO's COVID-19 vaccine insurance scheme for poor countries?

A vaccine scheme co-led by WHO is setting up a compensation fund for people in 92 low-income countries who might suffer any side-effects from COVID-19 vaccines. It would pay a lump sum based on the severity of the harm to victims from side-effects. It would be funded with a levy on vaccines distributed to poor countries and from other sources.

Slovakia begins testing all residents aged 10 & above for COVID-19

Slovakia on Saturday began testing all its residents aged 10 and above, approximately 40 lakh people, for coronavirus. Those who test positive will have to undergo a 10-day quarantine. Around 20,000 medical staff plus soldiers have been deployed at 5,000 sites to administer the antigen swab tests. Prime Minister Igor Matovic said he hopes it will avert a strict lockdown.

Some countries are on dangerous track, this is not a drill: WHO on COVID-19

WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus has said the world is now at a critical juncture in COVID-19 pandemic and some countries are on a dangerous track. "We urge leaders to take immediate action, to prevent further unnecessary deaths, essential health services from collapsing...As I said it in February and I'm repeating it today: This is not a drill," he added.
